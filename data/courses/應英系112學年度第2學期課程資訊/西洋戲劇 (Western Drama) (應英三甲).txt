課程教學目標：
1. gain knowledge about a variety of dramatic elements, theatrical techniques and conventions
2. enhance speaking skills and motivation via performing drama
3. experience and value drama as a social, collaborative and creative art form
4. develop comprehension abilities, analytical and synthesizing skills via drama
5. promote mutual collaboration through peer and group activities

課程教學綱要：
1. This course is designed to develop students’ reading and speaking skills through reading landmarks of drama and conducting collaborative drama activities. We cannot launch this introductory course to Western drama without tackling William Shakespeare, a must-read for all English majors. Among the Shakespearean plays, Romeo and Juliet presents challenging responses to the feudal-aristocratic society of medieval England and dovetails with the spirit of the Renaissance (14th~17th) that turns the eyes inward towards human nature and what constructs social order. Shakespeare’s works receive quite amount of movie adaptations, and we will read Romeo and Juliet with Shakespeare in Love, a 1998 film based on the play.
The second half of the semester will be devoted to Waiting for Godot, the award-winning work of Samuel Beckett, recipient of the 1969 Nobel Prize in Literature. His minimal theater lays bare of dramatic elements and theatrical techniques, and provides us with insights into the philosophical movement in the 20th century known as existentialism. Course reading materials along with the designed activities of drama performance will establish springboards for critical readings of literature, develop analytical reading skills, and produce critical responses. Teaching literature to adult language learners both enhance the effectiveness of language learning, and develop literary appreciation and literacy skills.

Students will be selected at random to answer questions and be graded based on how well they demonstrate understandings of the reading. No make-up quizzes for your absences. Understand and be responsible for your grade.
