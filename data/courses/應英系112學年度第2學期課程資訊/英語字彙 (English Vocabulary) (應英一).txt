課程教學目標：
1. experience the power of narration in short stories
2. familiarize students with narrative techniques and perspectives (ex:“interior/ internal monologue,”“unreliable narrator,”“omniscient or limited point of view”)
3. develop analytical skills on narrative structure and characterization
4. learn the wider contexts of short stories 

課程教學綱要：
1. This course guides students to read superb pieces of storytelling, to experience the power of narration in short stories and how contrived narratives intrigue readers, draw them in, or even shock them within episodes of character’s revelation, self-disclosure, and epiphany. What take part in this exploration are Edgar Allan Poe’s classic suspense stories: “A Tell-Tale Heart” (1843) and “Masque of the Red Death” (1842). The psychological suspense of Poe (1809-1849) stares into the darkest abyss of human nature and have readers witness scenes of bloody horror. Students will experience the storytelling masters’ distinctive narrative techniques and perspectives, writing styles as well as the recurring motifs and character growth in their works, and most important of all, enrich the interpretations of literature in dialogue with your own experiences.
