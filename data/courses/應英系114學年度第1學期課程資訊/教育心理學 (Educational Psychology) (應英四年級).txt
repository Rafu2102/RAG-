課程教學目標：
1. This course covers the diverse yet significant issues in Educational Psychology. The first half of the course aims to familiarize students with three theoretic perspectives of Educational Psychology: behaviorism, nativism, and constructionism, and the supplementary teaching strategies that can motivate students in their acquisition processes underlying the different perspectives. The second half of the course brings students to the extended yet deepened discussion on Educational Psychology (not limited to the context of learning classrooms). The issues include cognitive (self, social, and moral) development, learner differences and needs, first and second language acquisition, cultural diversity, and teaching pedagogies and assessments. Students are encouraged to consider their previous learning experiences in retrospect to sort out the theoretic differentiation. Furthermore, they must actively construct their own course syllabus (not limited to the English course) by contrasting or synthesizing different approaches and methods in the classroom in order to arrive at the more comprehensive understanding of Educational Psychology, or acquisition and teaching in general.

課程教學綱要：
1. The course outline includes: 1. Behavioral Views of Learning; 2. Cognitive Views of Learning; 3. Complex Cognitive Processes; 4. Constructivism and Designing Learning Environments; 5. Social Cognitive Views of Learning and Motivation; 6. Motivation in Learning and Teaching; 7. Cognitive Development; 8. The Self, Social, and Moral Development; 9. Learner Differences and Learning Needs; 10. Language Development, Language Diversity, and Immigrant Education; 11. Culture and Diversity; 12. Managing Learning Environments; 13. Teaching Every Student; 14. Classroom Assessment, Grading, and Standardized Testing.
