課程教學目標：
1. To enhance students' ability to critically analyze and interpret a wide range of readings on complex themes
2. To facilitate discussions on contemporary issues related to border-crossing, sustainability and cultural heritage
3. To develop students' abilities to articulate their ideas and findings effectively in both written and verbal forms
4. To inspire interest in learning about and addressing the critical challenges facing our world today

課程教學綱要：
1. This course guides students through the autobiographical writing of the renowned Mexican-American author Francisco Jiménez, engaging them with contemporary critical issues surrounding border-crossing. The class focuses on U.S.-Mexico borderland issues, self-alienation under capitalism, labor exploitation, social mobility, and postmemory. It encourages students to explore diverse voices from the margins and deepen their understanding of these critical issues.

成績評定方式：

課堂要求：
Students are expected to attend all class sessions and come prepared with the required course materials. Late arrival, failure to bring course materials, or engaging in distractions such as phone use (“phubbing”) will result in deductions from the final grade. Each group discussion counts toward your participation grade; active engagement is essential. Punctuality is mandatory. Students may be called on at random to answer questions and will be graded based on how well they demonstrate understanding of the assigned readings. There are no make-up quizzes for absences. It is your responsibility to understand the course requirements and manage your performance accordingly.
