課程教學目標：
1. Develop multimodal competence and literacy by producing multimodal texts that incorporate linguistic, auditory, gestural, spatial, and visual elements.
2. Create multimodal compositions that effectively combine various modes to convey meaning.
3. Write efficient descriptions that "show" rather than "tell"—helping readers visualize and experience the text vividly.
4. Understand and apply the power of poetic and descriptive devices, including a) Line breaks, b) Strong action verbs, c) Rhyme, d) Imagery and Metaphors, e) Sensory details.
5. Write collaboratively and self-expressively, engaging with peers to refine ideas and develop voice.
6. Gain knowledge of the structures and forms of English poetry and learn to adapt these forms with creativity and flexibility.
7. Experience the full range of the writing process, including Brainstorming > Drafting > Presenting > Peer reviewing > Revising for publication.

課程教學綱要：
1. Designed for advanced ESL writers, this writing course introduces a unique literary genre, poetry, to facilitate the goal that writing serves as both a social means of self-representation and a personal tool to inquire into real-life issues. Throughout this semester students will read a variety of poems, composed by writers of different language backgrounds, analyze poetic devices in these creative works, and adapt to these with creativity in their own poems. At the end of this class students will compose a collection of poems that reflect their unique perspectives. The ultimate goal of this class is to allow students to utilize writing as a dialogic thinking space and to have a sense of ownership in the learning experience.

課堂要求：
Students are expected to attend all class sessions and come prepared with the required course materials. Late arrival, failure to bring course materials, or engaging in distractions such as phone use (“phubbing”) will result in deductions from the final grade. Each group discussion counts toward your participation grade; active engagement is essential. Punctuality is mandatory. Students may be called on at random to answer questions and will be graded based on how well they demonstrate understanding of the assigned readings. There are no make-up quizzes for absences. It is your responsibility to understand the course requirements and manage your performance accordingly.
