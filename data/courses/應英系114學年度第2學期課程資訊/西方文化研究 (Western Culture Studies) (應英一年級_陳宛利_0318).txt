課程教學目標：
1. develop sensibility and academic literacy about American cultures
2. be attuned to notice the identity politics of language in relation to ethnicity
3. build cross-cultural competence and awareness in this rapidly globalized world
4. foster communication and presentation skills

課程教學綱要：
1. This course examines key elements of Western culture, with an emphasis on major aspects of American culture. Students will read curated articles and participate in in-depth discussions on topics such as traditional American values, the concept of the American Dream, language and ethnic identity, and more. Classroom discussions will revolve around the assigned readings and include an analysis of American cultural representations in literature and film. Through these activities, students will gain a deeper understanding of the United States and its cultural dynamics.

課堂要求：
Students are expected to attend all the class sessions and attend each class with course materials. Late arrival, coming to class empty-handed, or being a phubber in class will lead to a deduction of the final grade. Be on time and come prepared to learn and contribute. Students will be selected at random to answer questions and be graded based on how well they demonstrate understandings of the reading. No make-up quizzes for your absences. Understand and be responsible for your grade. To facilitate learning outside the classroom, course materials and assignments will be uploaded onto E-campus, an E-learning environment for all NQU courses. All students must resister for E-campus and also enter a frequently used email account in order to gain full access to course materials, to receive course updates, to form small groups, and to submit homework online. In an early prevention of an unexpected internet breakdown, please be advised to download weekly reading materials in advance. For course-related questions, contact me at tmpmlily@nqu.edu.tw
