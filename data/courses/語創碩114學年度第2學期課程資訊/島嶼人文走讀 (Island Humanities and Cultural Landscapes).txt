課程教學目標：
1. This course employs interdisciplinary research and place-based field studies (Humanities Walks) to guide students through a deep exploration of island environments, historical trajectories, settlement patterns, traditional heritage, and contemporary social issues. At its core, the course treats "Humanities" and "Landscape" as an indissoluble whole. It aims to cultivate students who possess both a macro-perspective of cultural landscapes and a micro-mastery of local knowledge.

課程教學綱要：
1. Practical Inquiry & Field Skills: Students will synthesize local studies literature with outdoor field investigations to master practical skills in systematic observation, documentation, interviewing, and problem identification.
2. Interdisciplinary Critical Thinking: By integrating knowledge from the humanities, geography, and ecology, students will develop the capacity to critically analyze the historical and contemporary transformations of island landscapes.
3. Local Engagement & Social Practice: Students will translate their findings into actionable proposals for cultural preservation and sustainable development, fostering a deep commitment to local social practice.

成績評定方式：
1. Active seminar participation & presentation: 60%
2. Final project: 40% (Working as local guides, students will present a Live Interpretive Tour along a self-designed route. This is paired with an actionable proposal—a comprehensive plan that offers solutions for heritage preservation or sustainable development in Jinmen.)

課堂要求：
本課程為主動學習研討會（Active learning seminar），而非傳統講授式課程。學生的首要任務是擔任「地景轉譯者」（Landscape Translator），需以主題小組協作方式，解構每週指定閱讀，並與田野實地觀察進行整合。課程最終將以接力式導覽形式呈現，由學生引導同儕對金門的冷戰與生態地景進行詮釋。學生的表現取決於其將學術理論（例如宋怡明的社會動員理論）與田野現場所發掘的微觀敘事進行連結與跨越的能力。
參與實地考察的同學，請於行程前自行辦理個人旅遊平安保險事宜。
