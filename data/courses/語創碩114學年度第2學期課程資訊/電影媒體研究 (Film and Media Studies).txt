課程教學目標：
1. This course investigates the social, cultural, ecological, commercial, and historical forces that shape cinema. Students will critically analyze film narrative structures and evaluate the medium's evolving role within the broader cultural and media ecology.

課程教學綱要：
1. Demonstrate a command of core concepts and terminology in film and media studies.
2. Contextualize a diverse body of cinematic works within their historical, cultural, and theoretical frameworks.
3. Apply critical methodologies and analytical tools to deconstruct cinematic techniques and narrative structures.
4. Construct coherent, evidence-based arguments about film through formal academic writing.

成績評定方式：
1. Class Participation, Discussion, and Presentation 60%
2. Final Exam 40% (The final exam works like a graduate bibliography exam—but instead of testing your knowledge of books, it focuses on your grasp of the cinematic texts (movies) and our in-class analyses.)

課堂要求：
This course is not lecture-based. You will be expected to work individually and in groups to digest the weekly readings, and to develop the skills necessary to interpret and apply this knowledge.
