課程教學目標：
1. Foster the ability to reflect on personal experiences and navigate life transitions with greater awareness and insight. 2. Strengthen the skills needed to approach new challenges with confidence and resilience, both in professional and academic settings. 3.Equip students with the tools to understand and navigate life's challenges, supporting their personal growth and adaptability. 4. Promote the ability to engage in thoughtful, constructive discussions on significant life topics, fostering personal development and diverse perspectives.

課程教學綱要：
1. This course is designed for soon-to-be graduates, preparing them for their careers or further academic pursuits during the transition from college to post-college life. It consists of two main components: reading life narratives and building self-confidence in this threshold stage as they embark on new journeys. Students will engage with selected life writings and participate in discussions on topics such as the pursuit of happiness, love, gratitude, and meanings in life. The course materials aim to help students reflect on their lives and gain the confidence needed to face future challenges.

課堂要求：
Students are expected to attend all the class sessions and attend each class with the course materials. Late arrival, coming to class empty-handed, or being a phubber in class will lead to a deduction of the final grade. Be on time and come prepared to learn and contribute. Students will be selected at random to answer questions and be graded based on how well they demonstrate understandings of the reading. Understand and be responsible for your grade.
To facilitate learning outside the classroom, course materials and assignments will be uploaded onto E-campus, an E-learning environment for all NQU courses. All students must resister for E-campus and Zuvio, and also enter a frequently used email account in order to gain full access to course materials, to receive course updates, to form small groups, and to submit homework online. In an early prevention of an unexpected internet breakdown, please be advised to download weekly reading materials in advance. For course-related questions, contact me at tmpmlily@email.nqu.edu.tw
