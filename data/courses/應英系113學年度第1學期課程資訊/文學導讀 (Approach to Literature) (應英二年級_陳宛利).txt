課程教學目標：
1. To gain knowledge about a variety of poetic forms, dramatic elements, and literary techniques
2. To enhance speaking skills and motivation through performing dramatic readings and recitations of poetry
3. To experience and value poetry and drama as social, collaborative, and creative art forms
4. To develop comprehension abilities, analytical, and synthesizing skills through the study of poetry and drama
5. To promote mutual collaboration through peer and group activities in the analysis and performance of poetry and drama

課程教學綱要：
1. This course is designed to immerse students in the rich and varied world of poetry and drama, offering a comprehensive exploration of poetic forms, dramatic elements, and literary techniques. Through a blend of theoretical study and practical application, students will gain a deep understanding of these artistic expressions and their significance in both historical and contemporary contexts.

課堂要求：
Students are expected to attend all the class sessions and attend each class with course materials. Late arrival, coming to class empty-handed, or being a phubber in class will lead to a deduction of the final grade. Be on time and come prepared to learn and contribute. Students will be selected at random to answer questions and be graded based on how well they demonstrate understandings of the reading. No make-up quizzes for your absences. Understand and be responsible for your grade. To facilitate learning outside the classroom, course materials and assignments will be uploaded onto E-campus, an E-learning environment for all NQU courses. All students must register for E-campus and also enter a frequently used email account in order to gain full access to course materials, to receive course updates, to form small groups, and to submit homework online. In an early prevention of an unexpected internet breakdown, please be advised to download weekly reading materials in advance. For course-related questions, contact the instructor at tmpmlily@nqu.edu.tw
