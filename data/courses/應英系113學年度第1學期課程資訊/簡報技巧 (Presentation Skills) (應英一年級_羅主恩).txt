課程教學目標：
1. Course Description: The aim of this course is to provide students with the necessary skills, techniques and methods to be effective in presenting in front of an audience. In addition to this, the course helps participants streamline and improve their performance through the observation and analysis of their own presentations and those of others. Promote further development of communication skills, quantitative analysis, critical thinking, and methods of presenting varying levels of technological information. The course focus will be the close examination of the elements that characterize an efficient, thorough, clear and effective presentation. Equal emphasis will be on the extensive building of vocabulary.

課程教學綱要：
1. Objectives: Students will:
- develop the ability to read critically and recognize the qualities of technical and scientific writings;
- examine the elements of a quality oral presentation by:
a. describing and analyzing the structure of an oral presentation;
b. determine what background information, if any, should be included;
c. determine the visual aids that would enhance the presentation;
Activities: Selected readings will be summarized and discussed for content, adding, and interpreting technical terminology, concepts, and methodology. Students will be required to research topic and determine what background information is needed for better understanding both for the speaker as well as the audience. Students will develop group presentations during the course of the semester. A written presentation will be required in addition to an outline. Visual materials will be a part of the assignment grade.

成績評定方式：
1. Evaluation: 30%-mid-term examination; 30%-final examination; class participation 40% (Attendance, tardiness, participation in class discussions and group oral reports, quizzes).

課堂要求：
Requirements: 
1. Attendance, punctuality. Quizzes, other assignments. 
2. Group presentations (10 -12 minutes).
3. ALL presentations and assignments are due on the specified date. Points will be deducted for late work. No late presentations will be permitted. 
4. The Midterm Exam will be a written test that will cover Steps 1 through 4 in the textbook. The Final Exam will be accumulative, but will have fewer questions from the first half of the book.
