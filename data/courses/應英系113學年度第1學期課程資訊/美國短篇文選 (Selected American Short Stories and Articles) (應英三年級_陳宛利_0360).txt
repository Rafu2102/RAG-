課程教學目標：
1. To enhance students' ability to critically analyze and interpret a wide range of readings on complex themes
2. To develop students' abilities to articulate their ideas and findings effectively in both written and verbal forms
3. To facilitate discussions on contemporary issues related to sustainability and cultural heritage
4. To inspire interest in learning about and addressing the critical challenges facing our world today

課程教學綱要：
1. This course explores a diverse selection of American literature, focusing on critical themes such as the impact of modern advances and capitalism on sustainability, and the relationship between civilization, nature, and indigenous cultures. Through engaging activities, discussions, and projects, students will deepen their understanding of these complex issues, enhancing their analytical and critical thinking skills.

課堂要求：
Students are expected to attend all the class sessions and attend each class with course materials. Late arrival, coming to class empty-handed, or being a phubber in class will lead to a deduction of the final grade. Be on time and come prepared to learn and contribute. Students will be selected at random to answer questions and be graded based on how well they demonstrate understandings of the reading. No make-up quizzes for your absences. Understand and be responsible for your grade.
