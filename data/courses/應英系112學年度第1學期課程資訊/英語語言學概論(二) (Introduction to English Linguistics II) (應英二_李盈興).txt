課程教學目標：
1. This course is designed for students who are no linguists yet, but who will need some familiarity with the fundamentals of linguistic knowledge in order to help them practice their profession in the future. This includes specialties in such fields as anthropology, composition, education, English as a second language (ESL), experimental phonetics, folklore, foreign languages, literature, psychology, speech-language pathology (語言病理學), reading, and technical and business communication. Since this is an introductory course, students are oriented to “do” linguistics or “analyze” languages with a focus on English data, supplemented with Mandarin data. Student will thus review what they have already known or acquire the new patterns of both languages from a linguistic perspective. The course is organized as follows. The first half of the course covers the theoretic areas of pragmatics, semantics, syntax, morphology, and phonology, respectively. Once students have a basic understanding of these areas, they will have little trouble mastering the applied areas that overlap with their own field of specialization. The second half convers the applied areas that are the most important to the greatest number of neighboring fields: language processing, language variation, language change, first-language acquisition, second-language acquisition, written language, and the neurology of language.

課程教學綱要：
1. The content of this semester contains:
- Phonetics: the physical dimensions of speech sounds
- Phonology: the psychological system of speech sounds
- Language acquisition: how human beings acquire their mother tongues
- Psycholinguistics: how language is mentally represented and processed
- Language variation: how language differs across social domains
- Language change: how language changes over time
- Writing systems: how human beings use written forms to express language
