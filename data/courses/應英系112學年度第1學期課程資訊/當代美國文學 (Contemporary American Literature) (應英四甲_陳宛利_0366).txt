課程教學目標：
1. develop cross-cultural competence and awareness through literature
2. be attuned to notice the role of language in identity politics
3. investigate how each individual writer represents or responds to the spirit of the age
4. develop reading strategy, comprehension abilities, analytical and synthesizing skills
5. foster communication and presentation skills

課程教學綱要：
1. Drawing on the emerging field of border studies, this course includes indispensable works of various genres that portray diverse experiences of living in the U.S.-Mexico borderlands, and situates the discussion of those works within their cultural fabrics. Thematic focuses include U.S.-Mexico border culture, the construct of American multiculturalism, and language and identity politics. Course materials incorporate semi-autobiography, artistic performances and installations, and short stories, all of which pave the ways for us to construct a broad portrait of the United States’s attitude towards immigration and the current status of multiculturalism. Finding traces of the past in the present, this course argues for a distinctive national tradition as portrayed in the literary classics and the emerging classics. It is highly recommended that students read the Latinx and Chico/a literature with critical reflection of Taiwan’s future prospects as a multicultural country.

課堂要求：
Students are expected to attend all the class sessions and attend each class with the course materials. Late arrival, coming to class empty-handed, or being a phubber in class will lead to a deduction of the final grade. Be on time and come prepared to learn and contribute. Students will be selected at random to answer questions and be graded based on how well they demonstrate understandings of the reading. Understand and be responsible for your grade.
To facilitate learning outside the classroom, course materials and assignments will be uploaded onto E-campus, an E-learning environment for all NQU courses. All students must resister for E-campus and also enter a frequently used email account in order to gain full access to course materials, to receive course updates, to form small groups, and to submit homework online. In an early prevention of an unexpected internet breakdown, please be advised to download weekly reading materials in advance.
