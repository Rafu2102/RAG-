課程教學目標：
1. Write efficient descriptions that “show” the reader what is happening, rather than “tell” the reader (“show” rather than “tell”)
2. Learn the power of poetic and descriptive devices in allowing the reader to connect to the writing, including action verbs, rhyme, imagery, metaphors, and sensory details
3. Use poetry to foster reading fluency and oral presentation skills (poetry reading)
4. Write collaboratively and self-expressively
5. Learn the structures and forms of English poetry, and adapt to these with creativity
6. Experience various writing processes: brainstorming, prewriting, drafting, peer-reviewing, and revising

課程教學綱要：
1. Designed for advanced ESL writers, this writing course introduces a unique literary genre, poetry, to facilitate the goal that writing serves as both a social means of self-representation and a personal tool to inquire into real-life issues. Throughout this semester students will read a variety of poems, composed by writers of different language backgrounds, analyze poetic devices in these creative works, and adapt to these with creativity in their own poems. At the end of this class students will compose a collection of poems that reflect their unique perspectives. The ultimate goal of this class is to allow students to utilize writing as a dialogic thinking space and to have a sense of ownership in the learning experience.
