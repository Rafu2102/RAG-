課程教學目標：
1. Social science is an important course. All too often our educational system rushes students into specialisations before the students have an overall picture -- before they know where they want to go. Once they have an overall picture, specialisation is necessary, but to specialise before having an overall picture is unfair to students. Students who specialise too early don't develop a commonsense perspective; they aren't sensitive to the interrelationships and resonances among disciplines. At worst, they become slaves of their discipline's approach. At best, they have the wisdom to recognise that there are many approaches to a problem, but their lack of training forces them to recreat the wheel. Knowledge of the other disciplines would have saved them the trouble and been far more efficient. The general social science course is one of the most important courses students take in college, and in my view it is a necessary prerequisite to taking courses in specific disciplines. It puts those other courses in perspective.

課程教學綱要：
1. This introductory module aims to facilitate students with a foundation of knowledge of social science in general. It introduces basic scopes, approaches, research questions, and concepts and theories of each prominent disciplines that are normally viewed as parts of social science. By acquiring basic and fundamental knowledge of these important parts, students are expected to develop an integrated understanding of social science and society.
