課程教學目標：
1. This course delves into the rich and complex world of contemporary adolescent literature. It critically examines issues surrounding "young adult literature" within today’s culturally diverse reading landscape, exploring how both "adolescence" as a concept and "Young Adult fiction" as a genre are shaped by cultural, historical, and psychological forces. Students will gain foundational knowledge of the emergence of the genre and become familiar with some of its most well-known authors. Drawing on cutting-edge research in literary criticism, cultural studies, and cognitive psychology, we will analyze key themes, narrative strategies, and the evolving representation of young people in fiction from the 20th and 21st centuries. Topics will include identity formation, nationhood, transgression, gender and sexuality, trauma, the role of media and music, and the cognitive processes of reading. Through close textual analysis and interdisciplinary engagement, you will develop a nuanced understanding of this vibrant and influential literary field.

課程教學綱要：
1. Articulate key theoretical concepts in adolescence, youth culture, and YA literature.
2. Analyze diverse contemporary YA texts using various critical lenses (e.g., postcolonial, feminist, psychoanalytical, cognitive).
3. Discuss the historical development and literary representation of "adolescence."
4. Evaluate portrayals of complex issues like identity, trauma, gender, and social justice in YA fiction.
5. Conduct independent research and present findings effectively.
6. Engage critically in dialogues about YA literature's social and cultural significance.

課堂要求：
Active Learning is Crucial: You must come prepared to speak, debate, and present ideas regularly.
Balance of Skills: Balances oral/active learning with traditional academic writing.
Consistent Effort: Consistent effort throughout the semester (showing up prepared, contributing) will be more rewarding than cramming for a single final exam or paper.
Synthesis of Knowledge: The presentations and discussions will require you to synthesize weekly readings and engage with course concepts on an ongoing basis, feeding into the more focused analysis required for the term paper.
